Culture V/S Civilization

There is subtle difference between culture and civilization

Culture : Culture is the collective form of all the expressions of life.

Civilization : The subtle sense of refinement that we come across in the different expressions of life is called civilization.


For example:

Suppose some guests have arrived at our home. We may address them imperatively, “Come” or we may welcome them by saying, “Please come in”. Here, “Please” speaks of civilization or a civilized address because it has the subtle sense of refinement of the expression. The imperative word, “Come” bereft of warmth, only smacks of lack of culture. This imperative address is not a civilized address.

It has both good and bad sides. The subtle sense of refinement that we come across in the different expressions of life is called civilization.
All civilization is a culture. Only those culture which has subtle sense of refinement is civilization.
Civilization V/S Science

Science: That which teaches the proper use of material things is science.


Civilization has an intimate relation with science. The progress of science and civilization should be balanced. They progress together. But where scientific progress supersedes civilization there civilization meets waterloo. We have many examples regarding this from the history.

Examples

  1. For instance, so long as the scientific progress of Greece and Egypt did not supersede civilization, civilization prospered very well. But when the science occupied a higher position than civilization by growing ingredients of enjoyment up in plenty, the civilization of both countries got destroyed.

  2. In India from the Tantric period to the Gupta period, civilization and science progressed side by side and never did science enjoy greater prestige than civilization. The Gupta period was the golden period of India. After the end of the Gupta era, scientific progress was overlooked. This resulted in degeneration and downfall.

  3. In the Pathan period, there was neither the development of civilization nor progress of science, with the result that the progress of society was thwarted.
Spiritual Progress

If science and civilization are harmonized, spiritual or intuitional progress gets accelerated.


Where the development of civilization is utterly negligible and science gradually attains the apogee of development, science only paves the way for destruction instead of doing any good to humanity. And so, study and practice of science, though unavoidable, should not be given a higher place than civilization.


In the present age, civilization is on the wane due to science enjoying the pride of place. But developed as science is today, if civilization is pushed up again to the top, people can reach a greater height than ever before.
